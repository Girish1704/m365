### 1. PURPOSE

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical principles and standards of behavior expected of all Contoso Corporation employees, contractors, and representatives. It serves as a guide for making decisions that align with our values and legal obligations.

---

### 2. SCOPE

This Code applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Board members and executives
- Contractors and consultants
- Vendors acting on Contoso's behalf

---

### 3. OUR CORE VALUES

| Value | Description |

| **Respect** | We treat everyone with dignity and fairness |

### 4. WORKPLACE CONDUCT

#### 4.1 Respectful Workplace

Contoso is committed to maintaining a workplace free from:
- Harassment of any kind (sexual, verbal, physical)
- Discrimination based on race, color, religion, gender, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, or any protected characteristic
- Bullying, intimidation, or threatening behavior
- Retaliation against those who report concerns

#### 4.2 Diversity and Inclusion

We celebrate diversity and believe that different perspectives strengthen our company. All employees are expected to:
- Embrace and respect differences
- Create an inclusive environment
- Challenge bias when observed
- Support equal opportunity

#### 4.3 Health and Safety

Every employee has the right to a safe workplace. We require:
- Compliance with all safety policies and procedures
- Immediate reporting of hazards or unsafe conditions
- Drug and alcohol-free workplace (except company-sanctioned events)
- Violence-free environment

---

### 5. BUSINESS ETHICS

#### 5.1 Conflicts of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with Contoso's interests:
- Disclose any potential conflicts to your manager and HR
- Do not accept gifts valued over $100 from vendors or clients
- Do not engage in outside employment that conflicts with your role
- Recuse yourself from decisions where you have a personal stake

#### 5.2 Confidential Information

Protect Contoso's confidential information and trade secrets:
- Never share proprietary information with unauthorized parties
- Use confidential information only for legitimate business purposes
- Secure documents, devices, and passwords
- Honor confidentiality obligations after leaving Contoso

#### 5.3 Anti-Corruption and Bribery

Contoso maintains a zero-tolerance policy for corruption:
- Never offer or accept bribes in any form
- Comply with the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act and all applicable laws
- Document all business expenses accurately
- Report any suspected corrupt activities immediately

#### 5.4 Fair Competition

We compete fairly and ethically:
- Never engage in price-fixing or market allocation
- Do not misrepresent our products or disparage competitors unfairly
- Respect intellectual property rights
- Comply with antitrust laws

---

### 6. USE OF COMPANY RESOURCES

#### 6.1 Company Property

- Use company assets (equipment, software, vehicles) responsibly
- Protect assets from theft, damage, or misuse
- Limited personal use of company resources is permitted if it does not interfere with work

#### 6.2 Technology and Data

- Follow all IT security policies
- Do not install unauthorized software
- Protect passwords and access credentials
- Use company systems for legitimate business purposes
- Understand that company systems may be monitored

#### 6.3 Social Media

- Do not speak on behalf of Contoso without authorization
- Keep confidential information private
- Be respectful and professional in all online interactions
- Clearly distinguish personal opinions from company positions

---

### 7. FINANCIAL INTEGRITY

#### 7.1 Accurate Records

- Maintain accurate and complete business records
- Never falsify documents, reports, or expense claims
- Retain records according to company policy
- Cooperate with audits and investigations

#### 7.2 Insider Trading

- Do not trade Contoso stock based on material non-public information
- Observe blackout periods as communicated by Legal
- Do not share insider information with others ("tipping")

---

### 8. EXTERNAL RELATIONSHIPS

#### 8.1 Customer Relations

- Treat customers with respect and professionalism
- Be honest about our products and capabilities
- Protect customer data and privacy
- Respond promptly to concerns and complaints

#### 8.2 Vendor Relations

- Select vendors based on merit, not personal relationships
- Ensure fair and competitive bidding processes
- Do not accept inappropriate gifts or entertainment
- Hold vendors to our ethical standards

#### 8.3 Government Relations

- Be truthful in all government interactions
- Comply with lobbying laws and reporting requirements
- Do not make political contributions on behalf of Contoso without approval

---

### 9. REPORTING CONCERNS

#### 9.1 Speak Up

If you observe or suspect a violation of this Code:
1. Report to your manager
2. Contact Human Resources
3. Use the Ethics Hotline (anonymous option available)
4. Email: ethics@contoso.com

**Ethics Hotline:** 1-800-555-ETHX (3849)  
Available 24/7, anonymous reporting available

#### 9.2 Non-Retaliation

Contoso strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports concerns in good faith. Retaliation will result in disciplinary action.

#### 9.3 Investigation Process

All reports will be:
- Taken seriously and investigated promptly
- Handled confidentially to the extent possible
- Resolved fairly with appropriate action

---

### 10. ENFORCEMENT

Violations of this Code may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Mandatory training
- Demotion or reassignment
- Termination of employment
- Legal action where appropriate

Severity of consequences depends on the nature and circumstances of the violation.
