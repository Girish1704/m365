# CONTOSO CORPORATION

## WORKPLACE HEALTH AND SAFETY POLICY

**Document Number:** HR-POL-008  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Last Reviewed:** November 15, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Facilities and Safety Department  
**Approved By:** Robert Williams, Chief Operations Officer

---

### 1. PURPOSE

Contoso Corporation is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment for all employees, contractors, visitors, and customers. This policy establishes the framework for maintaining workplace safety and outlines the responsibilities of all parties in creating a hazard-free environment.

---

### 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to:
- All Contoso Corporation premises and facilities
- All employees, contractors, and temporary workers
- Visitors to Contoso locations
- Company-sponsored events and activities
- Remote work environments (applicable sections)

---

### 3. POLICY STATEMENT

Contoso Corporation will:
- Comply with all applicable health and safety laws and regulations
- Provide a workplace free from recognized hazards
- Provide appropriate training and resources for safe work practices
- Continuously improve safety performance
- Encourage employee participation in safety programs

---

### 4. RESPONSIBILITIES

#### 4.1 Management Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Senior Leadership** | Allocate resources, set safety priorities, lead by example |
| **Department Managers** | Implement safety policies, conduct risk assessments, train employees |
| **Supervisors** | Monitor daily safety practices, address hazards, report incidents |

#### 4.2 Employee Responsibilities

All employees are expected to:
- Follow all safety policies, procedures, and instructions
- Use personal protective equipment (PPE) when required
- Report hazards, unsafe conditions, and incidents immediately
- Participate in safety training programs
- Not operate equipment without proper training
- Maintain a clean and organized work area
- Not work under the influence of drugs or alcohol

#### 4.3 Safety Committee

The Safety Committee meets monthly to:
- Review incident reports and trends
- Conduct facility inspections
- Recommend policy improvements
- Plan safety awareness initiatives

---

### 5. HAZARD IDENTIFICATION AND RISK ASSESSMENT

#### 5.1 Risk Assessment Process

1. **Identify** potential hazards in the workplace
2. **Assess** the likelihood and severity of harm
3. **Control** risks through elimination, substitution, or mitigation
4. **Document** findings and actions taken
5. **Review** regularly and after any incidents

#### 5.2 Common Workplace Hazards

| Hazard Type | Examples | Controls |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| **Ergonomic** | Repetitive strain, poor posture | Ergonomic equipment, training |
| **Slip/Trip/Fall** | Wet floors, cables, uneven surfaces | Signage, cable management, maintenance |
| **Electrical** | Faulty equipment, overloaded outlets | Regular inspections, certified repairs |
| **Fire** | Flammable materials, blocked exits | Fire systems, training, drills |
| **Chemical** | Cleaning products, lab materials | SDS, proper storage, PPE |

### 6. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

#### 6.1 Emergency Contacts

| Emergency | Contact |
|-----------|---------|
| **Life-threatening emergency** | 911 |
| **Security (24/7)** | +1 (555) 123-4571 |
| **Facilities Emergency** | +1 (555) 123-4572 |
| **HR Emergency Line** | +1 (555) 123-4573 |

#### 6.2 Fire Emergency

**If you discover a fire:**
1. **ALERT** - Activate nearest fire alarm
2. **EVACUATE** - Leave via nearest safe exit
3. **CALL** - Dial 911 from a safe location
4. **REPORT** - Meet at designated assembly point
5. **WAIT** - Do not re-enter until cleared by authorities

**Assembly Points:**
- Building A: North Parking Lot
- Building B: East Garden Area
- Building C: Main Entrance Plaza

#### 6.3 Medical Emergency

1. Call 911 for life-threatening situations
2. Contact Security at +1 (555) 123-4571
3. Do not move injured person unless in immediate danger
4. Locate nearest first aid kit and AED
5. Wait with the person until help arrives

#### 6.4 Evacuation Procedures

- Know your two nearest exits
- Do not use elevators during evacuations
- Assist colleagues with disabilities
- Proceed to designated assembly point
- Report to your department's safety warden

---

### 7. INCIDENT REPORTING

#### 7.1 What Must Be Reported

- All workplace injuries and illnesses
- Near-miss incidents
- Property damage
- Hazardous conditions
- Safety concerns

#### 7.2 Reporting Process

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Immediately** | Report to supervisor and seek medical attention if needed |
| **Within 24 hours** | Complete online incident report (Safety Portal) |
| **Within 48 hours** | Manager reviews and submits to Safety Team |
| **Within 5 days** | Investigation completed and corrective actions identified |

#### 7.3 Non-Retaliation

Contoso prohibits retaliation against any employee who reports safety concerns or participates in safety investigations. Report any retaliation to HR immediately.

---

### 8. WORKPLACE SECURITY

#### 8.1 Access Control

- Wear ID badge visibly at all times
- Do not share access cards or codes
- Report lost or stolen badges immediately
- Escort all visitors while on premises
- Challenge or report unknown individuals

#### 8.2 Workplace Violence Prevention

Contoso maintains zero tolerance for:
- Physical violence or threats
- Verbal abuse or intimidation
- Harassment or stalking
- Possession of weapons on premises

Report concerns to Security or HR immediately.

---

### 9. ERGONOMICS PROGRAM

#### 9.1 Workstation Setup

Employees should maintain proper ergonomics:

| Component | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Monitor** | Top of screen at eye level, arm's length away |
| **Keyboard** | Elbows at 90 degrees, wrists neutral |
| **Chair** | Feet flat on floor, back supported |
| **Lighting** | Adequate, no glare on screens |

#### 9.2 Ergonomic Assessments

Request an ergonomic assessment through Facilities if you experience:
- Discomfort during work
- Repetitive strain symptoms
- Need for specialized equipment

---

### 10. FIRST AID

#### 10.1 First Aid Kits

First aid kits are located:
- Each floor near elevators
- Break rooms and cafeteria
- Reception areas
- All company vehicles

Report used or missing supplies to Facilities.

#### 10.2 Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs)

AEDs are located:
- Building lobby areas
- Fitness center
- Large meeting rooms

Training is available through Safety Team.

---

|----------|-----------|----------|

| Ergonomics Awareness | Upon hire + as needed | All employees |

---

### 12. SUBSTANCE ABUSE

#### 12.1 Policy

Contoso prohibits:
- Working under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs
- Possession of illegal substances on company property
- Misuse of prescription medications affecting work safety
- Consumption of alcohol during work hours (except approved events)

#### 12.2 Support

Employees struggling with substance abuse are encouraged to seek confidential support through the Employee Assistance Program (EAP):
- Phone: 1-800-555-0199
- Available 24/7

---

### 13. REMOTE WORK SAFETY

Employees working remotely should:
- Maintain a safe, ergonomic home workspace
- Keep work area free from trip hazards
- Have working smoke detectors
- Report work-related injuries immediately
- Request ergonomic assessment if needed

---

### 14. POLICY COMPLIANCE

Failure to comply with safety policies may result in:
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Suspension
- Termination

Serious safety violations may result in immediate termination.
