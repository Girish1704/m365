### 1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes Contoso Corporation's performance management framework, including the review process, evaluation criteria, and performance improvement procedures. Effective performance management drives employee growth, organizational success, and ensures alignment with company goals.

---

### 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all regular full-time and part-time employees of Contoso Corporation who have completed their probationary period (90 days). Temporary and contract employees are reviewed according to their contract terms.

---

### 3. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT PHILOSOPHY

At Contoso, we believe in:

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Continuous Feedback** | Ongoing conversations, not just annual reviews |
| **Goal Alignment** | Individual goals connected to team and company objectives |
| **Development Focus** | Emphasis on growth and learning |
| **Two-Way Dialogue** | Employee input is valued and encouraged |
| **Fair Assessment** | Objective evaluation based on measurable outcomes |

### 5. GOAL SETTING (SMART GOALS)

Each employee should establish **3-5 annual goals** using the SMART framework:

| Element | Definition |
|---------|------------|
| **S**pecific | Clearly defined and unambiguous |
| **M**easurable | Quantifiable with success metrics |
| **A**chievable | Realistic given resources and constraints |
| **R**elevant | Aligned with role and company priorities |
| **T**ime-bound | Has a clear deadline or timeline |

**Goal Categories:**
1. **Performance Goals** (60-70%): Job-specific deliverables and outcomes
2. **Development Goals** (20-30%): Skills, competencies, career growth
3. **Company/Team Goals** (10-20%): Contribution to broader initiatives

---

### 6. RATING SCALE

Contoso uses a **5-point rating scale** for performance evaluations:

| Rating | Label | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **5** | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds all expectations; role model |
| **4** | Exceeds Expectations | Frequently exceeds expectations; strong contributor |
| **3** | Meets Expectations | Consistently meets expectations; solid performer |
| **2** | Needs Improvement | Occasionally meets expectations; development needed |
| **1** | Unsatisfactory | Does not meet expectations; immediate improvement required |

**Rating Distribution Guidelines:**
- Rating of 5: ~5-10% of employees
- Rating of 4: ~25-30% of employees
- Rating of 3: ~50-55% of employees
- Rating of 2-1: ~10-15% of employees

---

### 7. EVALUATION CRITERIA

Performance is evaluated across two dimensions:

#### 7.1 Results (What)
- Achievement of goals and objectives
- Quality of work and deliverables
- Meeting deadlines and commitments
- Business impact and contributions

#### 7.2 Behaviors (How)
Alignment with Contoso competencies:

| Competency | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Collaboration** | Works effectively with others |
| **Communication** | Expresses ideas clearly and listens actively |
| **Customer Focus** | Prioritizes customer needs and satisfaction |
| **Innovation** | Seeks creative solutions and improvements |
| **Accountability** | Takes ownership and delivers results |
| **Leadership** (for managers) | Develops and inspires team members |

---

### 8. REVIEW PROCESS

#### 8.1 Self-Assessment

Employees complete a self-assessment including:
- Progress against each goal with evidence
- Key accomplishments and contributions
- Challenges faced and lessons learned
- Development needs and career aspirations

#### 8.2 Manager Evaluation

Managers provide evaluation including:
- Assessment of goal achievement
- Rating on each competency
- Overall performance rating
- Specific feedback and examples
- Development recommendations

#### 8.3 Calibration

Leadership conducts calibration sessions to:
- Ensure rating consistency across teams
- Discuss high performers and development needs
- Make final rating decisions
- Identify succession candidates

#### 8.4 Performance Conversation

Managers conduct a formal review meeting to:
- Share the final rating and rationale
- Discuss accomplishments and areas for growth
- Set expectations for the coming year
- Create a development plan

---

### 9. PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (PIP)

For employees rated "Needs Improvement" or "Unsatisfactory":

#### 9.1 PIP Components

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Duration** | Typically 30-90 days |
| **Goals** | Specific, measurable improvement objectives |
| **Support** | Resources, training, coaching provided |
| **Check-ins** | Weekly or bi-weekly progress meetings |
| **Consequences** | Clear outcomes if not improved |

#### 9.2 PIP Outcomes

At the end of the PIP period:
- **Successful:** Employee returns to regular status
- **Extended:** Additional time granted if progress shown
- **Unsuccessful:** Employment may be terminated

---

### 10. COMPENSATION LINK

Performance ratings influence:

#### 10.1 Merit Increases

| Rating | Typical Merit Increase |
|--------|----------------------|
| 5 - Exceptional | 5-7% |
| 4 - Exceeds Expectations | 3-5% |
| 3 - Meets Expectations | 2-3% |
| 2 - Needs Improvement | 0% |
| 1 - Unsatisfactory | 0% |

*Actual increases depend on budget and market conditions.*

#### 10.2 Bonus Eligibility

| Rating | Bonus Eligibility |
|--------|------------------|
| 5 - Exceptional | Up to 150% of target |
| 4 - Exceeds Expectations | 100-125% of target |
| 3 - Meets Expectations | 75-100% of target |
| 2 - Needs Improvement | 0-50% of target |
| 1 - Unsatisfactory | Not eligible |

---

### 11. SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES

#### 11.1 New Employees

Employees with less than 6 months in role receive a "development" rating (no impact on compensation).

#### 11.2 Role Changes

Employees who changed roles mid-year receive input from both current and previous managers.

#### 11.3 Leave of Absence

Employees on extended leave are evaluated on their active work period only.

---

### 12. APPEALS PROCESS

Employees who disagree with their evaluation may:

1. Discuss concerns with their manager
2. Escalate to HR Business Partner
3. Request formal review by skip-level manager
4. Appeals must be submitted within **14 days** of receiving the review

---

### 13. MANAGER RESPONSIBILITIES

Managers are expected to:
- Set clear expectations at the start of the year
- Provide ongoing feedback (minimum monthly)
- Document performance throughout the year
- Conduct fair and objective evaluations
- Hold meaningful development conversations
- Complete all reviews on time
