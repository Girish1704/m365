### 1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes Contoso Corporation's commitment to maintaining a workplace free from harassment, discrimination, and retaliation. It defines prohibited conduct, outlines reporting procedures, and explains the investigation process.

- All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)

### 4. DEFINITIONS

#### 4.1 Harassment

Harassment is unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that:
- Creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment
- Unreasonably interferes with work performance
- Affects employment opportunities or decisions

**Examples of harassment include:**
- Slurs, epithets, or derogatory comments
- Offensive jokes or remarks
- Threatening or intimidating behavior
- Displaying offensive images, cartoons, or symbols
- Unwanted physical contact
- Mocking someone's accent, appearance, or beliefs
- Cyber-harassment or online bullying

#### 4.2 Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment is unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature, including:

**Quid Pro Quo:**
- Conditioning employment benefits on sexual favors
- Threatening negative consequences for rejecting advances

**Hostile Environment:**
- Unwelcome sexual advances or propositions
- Requests for sexual favors
- Sexual jokes, comments, or innuendos
- Displaying sexually explicit materials
- Unwanted touching, hugging, or physical contact
- Leering or inappropriate staring
- Sending sexually suggestive messages or images
- Comments about someone's body or appearance

#### 4.3 Discrimination

Discrimination is treating someone unfavorably because of a protected characteristic in:
- Hiring and recruitment
- Compensation and benefits
- Promotions and advancement
- Training and development
- Work assignments
- Performance evaluations
- Discipline and termination
- Any term or condition of employment

#### 4.4 Retaliation

Retaliation is any adverse action against someone who:
- Reports harassment or discrimination
- Participates in an investigation
- Opposes unlawful practices
- Exercises rights under this policy

**Examples of retaliation:**
- Termination or demotion
- Reduction in pay or hours
- Unfavorable performance reviews
- Exclusion from meetings or opportunities
- Hostile behavior or intimidation
- Spreading rumors or making false accusations

### 5. RESPONSIBILITIES

#### 5.1 All Employees

- Treat all individuals with dignity and respect
- Refrain from engaging in prohibited conduct
- Report witnessed or suspected violations
- Cooperate in investigations
- Complete required training

#### 5.2 Managers and Supervisors

All responsibilities above, plus:
- Model appropriate behavior
- Promptly report all complaints (even informal)
- Take immediate action to stop harassment
- Maintain confidentiality
- Prevent retaliation against reporters
- Complete management-specific training

#### 5.3 Human Resources

- Receive and respond to complaints
- Conduct impartial investigations
- Recommend corrective actions
- Maintain records and confidentiality
- Provide training and education
- Monitor workplace climate

#### 6.1 How to Report

Employees who experience or witness prohibited conduct should report promptly using any of these channels:

| Method | Contact Information |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Your Manager** | Direct conversation (unless involved) |
| **Human Resources** | hr@contoso.com or +1 (555) 123-4567 |
| **HR Business Partner** | Contact for your department |
| **Ethics Hotline** | 1-800-555-0140 (anonymous available) |
| **Online Portal** | ethics.contoso.com |
| **Senior Leadership** | Any VP or executive |

#### 6.2 Anonymous Reporting

- Anonymous reports are accepted via Ethics Hotline
- Provide as much detail as possible
- Anonymous reports may limit investigation options
- No retaliation for good-faith reports

#### 6.3 Information to Include

When reporting, include:
- Names of individuals involved
- Dates, times, and locations
- Description of what occurred
- Names of any witnesses
- Any documentation or evidence
- Prior reports or conversations about the issue

### 7. INVESTIGATION PROCESS

#### 7.1 Initial Assessment

Upon receiving a complaint, HR will:
1. Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. Assess the allegations
3. Determine appropriate investigation scope
4. Implement interim measures if needed

#### 7.2 Investigation Steps

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 1. **Interview Complainant** | Gather detailed account |
| 2. **Interview Accused** | Provide opportunity to respond |
| 3. **Interview Witnesses** | Collect additional information |
| 4. **Review Evidence** | Documents, emails, etc. |
| 5. **Analyze Findings** | Determine if policy violated |
| 6. **Recommend Action** | Propose appropriate response |
| 7. **Communicate Outcome** | Inform relevant parties |

#### 7.3 Investigation Principles

- **Prompt:** Investigations begin within 5 business days
- **Thorough:** All relevant evidence examined
- **Impartial:** Conducted without bias
- **Confidential:** Information shared only as necessary
- **Fair:** Both parties have opportunity to present information

#### 7.4 Timeframe

Most investigations are completed within 30 days. Complex cases may require additional time. All parties will be kept informed of progress.

---

### 8. INTERIM MEASURES

During an investigation, HR may implement interim measures:
- Temporary reassignment
- Schedule adjustments
- Work location changes
- Suspension with pay (pending investigation)
- No-contact directives

Interim measures are not disciplinary and do not imply guilt.

---

### 9. CORRECTIVE ACTION

If a violation is substantiated, corrective action will be taken, which may include:

| Severity | Potential Actions |
|----------|------------------|
| **Minor** | Verbal warning, coaching, training |
| **Moderate** | Written warning, mandatory training, transfer |
| **Serious** | Suspension, demotion, final warning |
| **Severe** | Immediate termination |

Corrective action is based on:
- Severity of the conduct
- Pattern or repeated behavior
- Position and responsibilities
- Impact on victim(s)
- Prior disciplinary history

---

### 10. CONFIDENTIALITY

Contoso will maintain confidentiality to the extent possible:
- Information shared only with those who need to know
- Participants must keep investigation details confidential
- Breaching confidentiality may result in discipline

Note: Complete confidentiality cannot be guaranteed when:
- Legal requirements mandate disclosure
- Investigation requires witness interviews
- Safety concerns exist

---

### 11. PROTECTION FROM RETALIATION

Contoso strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Makes a good-faith complaint
- Participates in an investigation
- Opposes discriminatory practices

**Retaliation is a serious offense that will result in discipline, up to and including termination, regardless of whether the underlying complaint is substantiated.**

If you experience retaliation, report it immediately.

---

### 12. FALSE ACCUSATIONS

While Contoso encourages reporting of all concerns, knowingly making false accusations is prohibited and may result in disciplinary action. This does not apply to good-faith reports that cannot be substantiated.
